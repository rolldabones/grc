# grc

This is the basic framework I use for governance, risk management and compliance (GRC), based on the GRC Capability Model from oceg.org.

## Core equation

**PP ≈ Ω(C | SP) ↑ UO**

## Definitions of variables and symbols

- **PP (Principled Performance):** The ultimate goal to reliably achieve objectives, address uncertainty, and act with integrity.
- **≈ (Is approached or achieved through):** Principled Performance emerges from the effective and sustained operation of the GRC system.
- **Ω (GRC Operational Cycle):** Continuous, iterative application of four components with feedback loops: **LEARN → ALIGN → PERFORM → REVIEW ↻**.
- **C (Set of GRC Capabilities/Elements):** The complete set of **20** functional elements used within **Ω**.
  - **Learn (L):** {L1 External Context, L2 Internal Context, L3 Culture, L4 Stakeholders}
  - **Align (A):** {A1 Direction, A2 Objectives, A3 Identification, A4 Analysis, A5 Design}
  - **Perform (P):** {P1 Controls, P2 Policies, P3 Communication, P4 Education, P5 Incentives, P6 Notification, P7 Inquiry, P8 Response}
  - **Review (R):** {R1 Monitoring, R2 Assurance, R3 Improvement}
- **| SP (Conditioned by / Initiated and focused by):** **Ω** is influenced and initially shaped by the specific starting point (**SP**): {SP0 Blank Canvas, SP1 Topical, SP2 Discipline, SP3 Element, SP4 Crisis}.
- **↑ UO (Guided and constrained by / Aspiring towards):** The entire GRC process is directed by and aims to achieve Universal Outcomes (**UO**).

## Universal Outcomes (expanded)

- **U1. Achieve Objectives that Produce and Preserve Value:** Ensure that strategy and execution prioritize objectives to produce and preserve value simultaneously.
- **U2. Balance Risk and Reward:** Ensure that opportunities and obstacles are adequately addressed so performance and risk remain acceptable.
- **U3. Improve Culture:** Establish a culture of total performance, accountability, integrity, trust, and communication across the organization.
- **U4. Enhance Stakeholder Confidence:** Provide assurance to stakeholders to increase confidence in mission, vision, values, and total performance.
- **U5. Integrate and Improve Decision-Making:** Integrate governance, management, assurance of performance, risk, compliance, and decision-making.
- **U6. Prevent, Detect, and Correct Undesired Conduct and Weaknesses:** Establish actions and controls to prevent, detect, recover from, and reduce negative effects of events.
- **U7. Promote, Detect, and Reward Desired Conduct and Strengths:** Establish actions and controls to promote, detect, increase, and compound positive effects of events.
- **U8. Sense and Respond to Context:** Proactively make sense of, predict, and address internal and external context changes to adjust strategy and tactics.
- **U9. Improve Total Performance:** Improve effectiveness, efficiency, agility, and resilience with proactive, detective, and responsive actions and controls.
- **U10. Honor and Express Values:** Balance pursuit of total performance while expressing and staying true to values, without sacrificing one for the other.

## Explanation of the equation

**PP** is not a simple sum. It is the outcome of the continuous and effective operation (**≈**) of **Ω**. Within **Ω**, the organization deploys needed capabilities (**C**) from the 20-element toolkit. The way the cycle begins and which capabilities are emphasized is conditioned by the organization’s starting point (**| SP**). The entire process is guided by and aspires toward (**↑**) the ten Universal Outcomes (**UO**), which keep GRC focused on achieving objectives, managing uncertainty, and acting with integrity.
