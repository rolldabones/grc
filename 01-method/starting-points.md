# Starting Points (SP)

Ω never starts from theory. It starts from a real situation, and the situation shapes which elements move first and which risks attend the entry. The Red Book names five starting points: **SP0 Blank Canvas, SP1 Topical, SP2 Discipline, SP3 Element, SP4 Crisis**. This document gives each an entry profile and a first-90-days sequence.

The plans below assume a capable owner with partial time and modest support. Scale up or down; do not reorder LEARN behind PERFORM without a reason you can defend.

---

## SP0 Blank Canvas

**Entry condition.** No formal capability exists. Work happens, obligations get met or missed, but nothing is designed.

**Risk of this entry.** Building the library before the house: months of frameworks and no operating control. The opposite risk is copying a peer's program wholesale, importing their context instead of learning yours.

**First 90 days.**
1. Days 1 to 20: L2 then L1. One internal context brief, one external context brief, a first authority source list. Prompts L2 and L1.
2. Days 21 to 40: A1 light and A2. A direction and appetite sketch with leadership; an objectives register with owners. Prompt A2, template `objectives-register`.
3. Days 41 to 65: A3 and A4. Objective-by-objective identification and a first calibrated assessment. Prompts A3 and A4, templates `risk-register` and `obligations-register`.
4. Days 66 to 90: A5 into P1 and P2 minimums, P6 open, R1 minimal. The ten controls that matter, a short policy set, a genuinely answered intake channel and a one-page dashboard.

**Good at day 90 looks like:** every artifact traceable to an objective; an open intake channel; a named owner for Ω.

## SP1 Topical

**Entry condition.** A topic is burning: AI, privacy, sanctions, anti-corruption, safety. Leadership wants the topic handled, not a program.

**Risk of this entry.** The topical capability becomes a silo with its own vocabulary and never generalizes; or the topic's urgency licenses skipping A2, so the work never attaches to objectives.

**First 90 days.**
1. Days 1 to 15: L1 scoped to the topic; obligations extraction. Prompt L1; build guide C as the working method.
2. Days 16 to 35: A3 and A4 scoped to the topic against existing enterprise objectives (write the two or three missing objectives if none exist; do not skip).
3. Days 36 to 70: A5 into P1 and P2 for the topic; P4 for the roles that touch it. For AI as the topic, run build guide D.
4. Days 71 to 90: R1 indicators for the topic; document the generalization path: which artifacts and habits extend to the next topic.

**Good at day 90 looks like:** the topic handled with artifacts a second topic could reuse.

## SP2 Discipline

**Entry condition.** A functioning discipline exists (risk management, compliance, internal audit or security) and the mandate is integration: one picture instead of three.

**Risk of this entry.** Turf. Integration read as takeover produces resistance and shadow registers. The second risk is vocabulary war: three functions, three definitions of risk.

**First 90 days.**
1. Days 1 to 20: A2 as the unifying spine. Put the enterprise objectives register at the center; every function maps its inventory to it.
2. Days 21 to 45: vocabulary reconciliation using `principled-performance.md` definitions as the neutral baseline; one identifier scheme (OBJ, RSK, OPP, OBL, CTL, POL, ISS).
3. Days 46 to 70: A4 recalibration: one scale set, jointly calibrated, applied across the merged registers. Prompt A4.
4. Days 71 to 90: R1 and R2 coordination: one indicator set, one assurance map. Template `review-assurance-plan`.

**Good at day 90 looks like:** one register spine, one vocabulary, one map; the functions keep their depth and lose their walls.

## SP3 Element

**Entry condition.** One element is visibly broken: policies are sprawl, the hotline is dead air, training is theater. The mandate is fix that.

**Risk of this entry.** Fixing the element in isolation reproduces the break, because most element failures are adjacency failures: policies sprawl because obligations are unmapped; hotlines die because culture punishes use.

**First 90 days.**
1. Days 1 to 10: locate the element in `elements.md`; read its feeds and fed-by lines; test each adjacency for the real break.
2. Days 11 to 55: rebuild the element with its prompt and template, repairing the one or two upstream adjacencies the diagnosis exposed.
3. Days 56 to 75: reconnect downstream: whoever consumes this element's outputs confirms they now receive them.
4. Days 76 to 90: R1 indicators on the rebuilt element; schedule an R2 look at it within two quarters.

**Good at day 90 looks like:** the element operating, its adjacencies flowing and a measure that would catch regression.

## SP4 Crisis

**Entry condition.** An incident, enforcement action, near-miss or public failure. Response is already running; the mandate arrives with adrenaline and a deadline.

**Risk of this entry.** Building a program shaped like the last crisis: maximal controls on the wound, nothing on the next exposure. The second risk is letting the urgency expire before the rebuild reaches ALIGN, leaving only scar tissue.

**First 90 days.**
1. Days 1 to 20: P6, P7 and P8 run properly and get documented: intake protected, inquiry scoped and fair (under counsel direction where applicable), response executed with closure evidence.
2. Days 21 to 40: root cause into A3 and A4: was this item identified, assessed and treated before it happened, and if not, why not. Assess the adjacent exposures the crisis illuminates, not just the one that fired.
3. Days 41 to 70: A5 redesign for the exposed family of items; P1 and P2 changes implemented with evidence.
4. Days 71 to 90: R1 indicators that would have detected this earlier; an R2 engagement scheduled on the redesigned area; L1 and L3 refresh, because crises usually reveal context or culture reads that were wrong.

**Good at day 90 looks like:** the file closable on evidence, the family of exposures treated and a monitoring net that would catch the recurrence.

## Choosing when several apply

Most real situations mix starting points. Choose by mandate, not by comfort: the SP is the one your sponsor would name. Then borrow: an SP1 entry uses SP0's day-1-to-20 moves in miniature; an SP4 entry becomes an SP2 integration once the file closes. The routing table in the repository README maps each SP to its first documents, prompts and templates.
