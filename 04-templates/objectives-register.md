# Template: Objectives Register

The spine of the capability. Every risk, opportunity, obligation and control ultimately traces to a row here. Element: A2. Prompt: `../03-prompts/A2-objectives.md`.

## Register

| OBJ-id | Objective | Owner | Unit | Parent OBJ | Measure | Target | Tolerance | Time frame | Linked RSK/OPP | Linked OBL | Review cadence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| OBJ-001 | Reduce order-to-cash cycle to 30 days | J. Park | Finance | OBJ-E02 | Days, monthly average | 30 | +5 days before escalation | FY2026 | RSK-014, OPP-003 | OBL-021 | Quarterly | Active |

## Field definitions

1. **OBJ-id.** Permanent identifier; never reused after retirement.
2. **Objective.** One measurable outcome per row. Compound statements are split.
3. **Owner.** One named person. Shared ownership is unowned.
4. **Parent OBJ.** The enterprise objective this cascades from; blank only at the top level.
5. **Measure, target, tolerance.** The metric, the intended value and the acceptable variation before escalation. Tolerance feeds R1 thresholds directly.
6. **Linked RSK/OPP and OBL.** Populated from A3 onward; an objective with no linked uncertainty after an identification pass is a flag, not a comfort.
7. **Status.** Active, At risk (tolerance breached), Achieved, Retired.

## Guidance

1. Both growth and preservation objectives belong here; a register that is all growth is blind on one side.
2. Retire objectives explicitly and keep the row; orphaned children reveal broken cascades.
3. An activity that traces to no objective is either missing its objective or is a candidate for stopping.
