# Template: Policy Document

The standard skeleton for the policy set. Element: P2. Prompt: `../03-prompts/P2-policies.md`. Build guide A governs the lifecycle around this document.

## Document skeleton

```
POL-id:              POL-000
Title:
Hierarchy level:     Policy | Standard | Procedure
Owner:
Approver:
Version:             0.1
Status:              Draft | In review | Approved | Published | Under revision | Retired
Effective date:
Next review date:
Linked OBL-ids:
Linked RSK-ids:
Linked CTL-ids:

1. PURPOSE
   Why this policy exists: the obligations, risks or decisions it implements,
   in two or three sentences.

2. SCOPE
   Who and what it covers, and any explicit exclusions.

3. DEFINITIONS
   Terms used with a controlled meaning. Align with the organization's
   vocabulary; define only what this document needs.

4. POLICY STATEMENTS
   4.1  Numbered, testable expectations. A reader can tell whether conduct
        complies with each statement.
   4.2  One expectation per number.

5. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
   Who does what under this policy, by role.

6. RELATED OBLIGATIONS AND CONTROLS
   The mapping table: statement number to OBL-id, RSK-id or decision
   reference, and the CTL-ids that enforce or evidence it.

7. EXCEPTIONS
   How exceptions are requested and approved; every exception carries an
   approver, an expiry and a compensating measure.

8. NON-COMPLIANCE
   Consequences, consistent with the organization's consequence framework.

9. REVIEW AND MAINTENANCE
   Review cycle (at least every two years), triggers for off-cycle review
   and where the authoritative version lives.

10. VERSION HISTORY
    | Version | Date | Author | Change summary | Approver |
```

## Guidance

1. Every statement in section 4 appears in section 6's mapping; untraceable statements are cut or justified before approval.
2. Plain language wins; a policy that needs a translator is not communicating expectations.
3. One authoritative location per policy; everything else links to it.
